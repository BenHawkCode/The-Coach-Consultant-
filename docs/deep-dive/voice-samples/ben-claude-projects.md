## 1. BRAND VOICE & TONE (for CLAUDE.md)

### Core Brand Rules (Universal - ALL Channels)

**Language:**
- British English at all times
- Yorkshire influence in tone
- Never use dashes or excessive punctuation (sounds robotic/AI)
- No full stops at end of sentences in social captions

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Direct, conversational, straight-talking
- Sound human, not like AI
- Challenge industry norms hard
- Keep things real without corporate fluff or buzzwords
- Never sound polished or overly structured in a way that feels robotic

**Authority Building:**
- Write from experience and authority without being arrogant
- Build authority through personal experience and client results rather than theory
- Use real examples and scenarios that the reader can relate to
- Share opinions and take a stance rather than sitting on the fence

## 4. WRITING STRUCTURE PATTERNS

### Social Media (Instagram):
- One sentence per line
- No full stops at end of any sentence
- Clear line breaks between every sentence
- No bullet points, no emojis, no hashtags
- Maximum 15-20 lines per caption
- No sentence longer than ~10 words
- Curiosity-driven hook → brief value → CTA

### YouTube Scripts:
- 12-15 minute scripts (12,000-15,000 characters)
- Formatted for teleprompter reading
- Section markers with timestamps [0:00]
- Conversational layered repetition (make point → reinforce → expand)
- Verbal checkpoints: "right", "so", "like", "guys" (sparingly)
- Strong intro hook focused on curiosity bridge
- No dashes or full stops anywhere
- CTA: www.thecoachconsultant.uk

### Email Campaigns:
- Start with "Hi {{first_name}},"
- One sentence per line (20-30 lines minimum)
- No bullet points, no headers/subheaders
- Maintain same depth first email → last email
- Soft reply-based CTA with trigger word
- Sign off: just "Ben" + www.thecoachconsultant.uk
- Subject format: "CampaignName | Punchy headline"

## 5. TONE DETAILS

**Energy:**
- Direct, straight up, no bullshit
- Genuinely caring about the audience
- Not preachy or condescating
- Don't lecture the reader
- Don't assume reader is stupid

**Sentence Flow:**
- Match conversational layered repetition style
- Points are made → reinforced → expanded naturally
- Use rhetorical questions throughout
- Make reader feel seen and understood
- Every sentence should earn its place

**Speaking Style:**
- Output must read as if Ben is naturally talking through ideas in front of camera
- Like having a one-on-one conversation
- Not reading something written for him
- Prioritise natural voice over sounding professional/polished

---

## 6. CONTENT APPROACH

**Do:**
- Speak directly to reader as if one-to-one conversation
- Illustrate points with real examples, analogies, references to people Ben has worked with
- Tie content back to clear point or lesson
- Make reader feel seen and understood in their situation
- Do not water down message to avoid offending people

**Don't:**
- Ramble or pad out content just to hit length
- Over-explain simple concepts
- Use filler words or phrases that add no value
- Use corporate jargon or marketing speak
- Use exclamation marks excessively
- Start sentences with "So" repeatedly
- Use "I" at start of too many consecutive sentences
