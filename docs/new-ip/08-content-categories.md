8. Content Categories



The Strategic Content Framework

Five Categories That Move Open Business Owners From Guessing To Predictable



Right, this is the doc you grab when you sit down to plan a week, a month, or a quarter of content. Five strategic categories. Each one does a specific job in the funnel - from "stop the scroll" to "book the audit". Used together they compound. Used in isolation they get diluted.



Target Audience Quick Reference



Sam (the avatar): open business owner, men and women, £100K-£500K/year, founder-led service business, currently guessing every growth decision because they have no AI intelligence behind their content, acquisition or sales.



Pain hierarchy (the order content speaks to):

The Guesswork Tax (hero)

The Bottleneck Identity (close second)

The AI Era Anxiety

The Trust Trauma

The Plate Anxiety



What they want from content:

A line that names a specific feeling they thought only they had

Real proof, not vague testimonials

Specific numbers, not "10x" hype

Mate-to-mate voice, not corporate

Process behind the polish



Primary Problem Breakdown



Three-tier structure for content angle planning:



Tier 1 - Hero Pain: The Guesswork Tax

Every decision is a guess. No AI intelligence behind any of it. Lead most ads, hero hooks, sales pages with this.



Tier 2 - Supporting Pain: The Bottleneck Identity

Everything runs through them. They cannot step back. Use as the backup angle when guesswork has been overused, or as the bridge to the predictability promise.



Tier 3 - Niche Pain: AI Era Paralysis

They know AI is the answer but cannot tell tools from intelligence. Use in content aimed at warmer audiences who already know they need AI.



The Five Strategic Content Categories



Category 1: Guesswork Exposure



Purpose:

Agitate the hero pain. Get the reader to admit "I'm guessing every decision in this business" before they're ready to admit it out loud. Create the moment of "oh god, that's me".



Core repeatable messages:

"Every decision in your business right now is a guess."

"You haven't got AI intelligence behind a single thing you do."

"Every campaign you run is a coin flip."

"Posting and praying isn't a content strategy."

"You're flying blind on what actually converts."

"Your gut got you here. Intelligence gets you next."



Weekly rotation strategy:

Monday: Guesswork tax case study (operator who was guessing, what changed)

Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes guesswork story with emotional impact

Friday: Cost-of-inaction breakdown using the £120K, 1,820 hours, £2,300/week stack



Proof to integrate:

Time-tracking data showing 1,820+ hours/year on guesswork-driven admin

Screenshots of real Claude AI Specialists answering "what's working in my business right now?"

Client case studies with specific revenue jumps after switching from guess to known

Real before-and-after of campaigns run on guess vs run on intelligence



Content hooks:

"Right, so at 11 PM last night I was scrolling and I realised something..."

"Here's what nobody admits about running their own business..."

"Three decisions you made this week that were probably just guesses..."

"You're not running a business. You're running a guessing machine."

Voice And Tone Consistency Guidelines



Signature language patterns (use these often):

"Right, so here's the thing..."

"Look, what used to be impossible is now inevitable..."

"The more intelligent you become, the more authentic you sound..."

"That's what I love about AI intelligence systems..."

"You just need to understand the difference between..."

"You are the bottleneck of your business."

"Stop guessing growth."



Authenticity markers:

British spellings and expressions throughout

Personal experience stories with specific details

Direct challenge of industry assumptions

Mate-to-mate conversational tone even in written content

No corporate jargon or buzzword-heavy language

Inclusive language (men and women equally)

Specific numbers, not vague claims



Content Quality Control System



Pre-publication checklist (run this every time):



Does it speak directly to Sam (the avatar)?

Does it lead with guesswork or bottleneck pain?

Am I using mate-to-mate voice patterns naturally?

Does it connect to the 4-step programme structure where logical?

Would someone immediately understand what makes us different?

Is there clear proof backing up the claims?

Does it use British English, no em-dashes?

Does it speak to men and women equally?

Does it avoid AI-sounding phrases ("transformation journey", "unlock", "dive in")?

Does it include a partner-friendly CTA, not high-pressure?



If yes to all, ship. If any are off, rewrite.



Monthly voice audit:

Review top-performing content for voice consistency patterns

Ensure AI intelligence messaging stays central across all categories

Verify emotional connection to Sam's specific reality

Maintain balance between supportive and challenging tone



Bottom line: this content framework is the conversion engine. Five categories that compound. Two posts per day in rotation. Every piece runs through the quality control checklist before publishing. Every month gets optimised against conversion data.



The framework's job is to systematically move open business owners from "I'm guessing every decision" to "I've booked the Growth Intelligence Audit call". Five categories. Five jobs. One spine.
