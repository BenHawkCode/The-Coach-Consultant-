4. Offer Positioning



The Ten-Step Positioning Playbook For The Coach Consultant



Right, this is the tactical positioning doc. Use this when you sit down to refine the offer, write a sales page, plan an ad campaign, or rebrief the team on what the offer actually is. Ten steps. Each one builds on the last. The whole thing is anchored on the same spine: stop guessing growth, build AI intelligence, copy-paste SOPs, personal brand plus AI adoption, done-with-you across 90 days.



Step 1: Define The Unique Value Proposition



The transformation we deliver that nobody else does:



Unique Method: AI intelligence systems plus copy-paste SOPs delivered done-with-you, not done-by-themselves through videos and a Slack channel.



Unique Result: Predictable growth. Specifically: AI intelligence behind every content, acquisition and sales decision, business owner out of the bottleneck, personal brand running consistently, two weeks off without panic.



Unique Approach: 4-step programme - Growth Intelligence Audit, Claude AI Specialist Setup, Personal Brand and AI Adoption Roadmap, 90-Day Growth Map. Sequenced. Specific. Built on Claude as the core stack.



Unique Guarantee: Built for you, used by you, owned by you. The systems and SOPs go with the operator forever, not rented through a subscription.



What makes us unavailable elsewhere:

The combination of AI intelligence (not just AI tools) and personal brand (not just content production)

Done-with-you delivery, not done-by-yourself

Founder-led by someone who rebuilt from a 2018 collapse and ran the playbook personally

Claude AI Specialist setup trained specifically on each operator's voice and methodology

The cost-of-inaction frame backed with real numbers (£120K, 1,820 hours, £2,300/week)



Step 2: Position Against The Industry's Failure Modes



Every category we operate in is broken in a specific way. Our positioning is the deliberate inverse of each.



Failure Mode 1: AI Tools Are Sold As Solutions

Industry default: "Buy this AI tool. It will change your business."

Our solution: We sell AI intelligence systems. Tools without intelligence are noise. Intelligence is the insight - what's working, what's not, what to do next.

Position: "You don't need more AI tools. You need actual intelligence."



Failure Mode 2: Generic Frameworks Get Resold To Everyone

Industry default: One framework, deployed identically across coaching/agency/consulting/health.

Our solution: Custom Claude AI Specialist trained on each operator's actual business and voice.

Position: "Frameworks just the same is over. Built for your business specifically."



Failure Mode 3: Course Delivery Pretends To Be Implementation

Industry default: Watch the videos, join the Slack, you'll figure it out.

Our solution: 90 days of done-with-you build alongside the operator. Real implementation, not optional homework.

Position: "Promised the world before? Done-with-you is the difference."



Failure Mode 4: Growth Promises Without Predictability Promises

Industry default: "10x your output. Scale. Grow."

Our solution: Predictability first. Then growth. Predictability is the new growth metric.

Position: "Stop guessing growth. Start knowing it."



Failure Mode 5: AI Replacement Anxiety, No Defence

Industry default: "AI is going to commoditise everything. Hope you're ready."

Our solution: Personal brand plus AI adoption as twin pillars of defence.

Position: "Two things separate winners right now: Personal Brand and AI Adoption."



Step 3: Choose The Price Positioning Strategy



Recommended position: Premium.



Why premium works for this market:

The buyers charge premium themselves. They understand it.

Time is their most valuable asset, and we're freeing it.

Done-with-you delivery legitimately costs more to deliver than course delivery.

Predictability of revenue is worth real money to them. Compare to the £120K cost of inaction.

Personal brand work is hard to commoditise - it commands a premium.



Premium pricing indicators we lean on:

Specific 4-step programme structure (named, branded, repeatable)

Custom Claude AI Specialist setup (high-effort, high-craft)

Real screenshots and named clients (signals the level)

Founder access and direct involvement (not a course with a coach attached)



Pricing strategy:

Position 30-50% above generic AI consulting offers

Stack the cost-of-inaction frame against the price (£120K/year of staying manual)

Bundle the full programme - resist the urge to itemise components, that frames it as transactional rather than transformational

Offer payment-flex on the call where genuine cash flow is the block, not framing

Never undercut the price as the lever to close - that signals the offer was overpriced

Step 8: Address Objections Pre-emptively



Address each objection in the page copy, the ad copy, the sales call opener - before the prospect raises it.



Objection 1: "I cannot add more to my plate."

Pre-empt: "This doesn't add to your plate. The first deliverable is an audit that tells you what to STOP doing. The 90 days that follow LIGHTEN your plate every week."



Objection 2: "I've been burnt by mentors before."

Pre-empt: "If you've been promised the world before, you're going to be sceptical of this too. Good. Done-with-you is fundamentally different. Specific deliverables. Named programme. No Slack-channel ghosting. Built for you, used by you, owned by you."



Objection 3: "What if AI changes again next year?"

Pre-empt: "We build adaptive systems on Claude that evolve with the model, not fragile setups dependent on one tool. The methodology outlives the tool."



Objection 4: "My partner needs to be on board."

Pre-empt: "Bring them. We expect the partner on the second call by default. Decisions like this stall when one of you isn't in the conversation."



Objection 5: "I cannot afford it right now."

Pre-empt: "Stack it against the £120K hidden cost of staying manual for another year. Then re-ask the question. If cash flow is genuinely the block, we'll talk through structure - but most of the time it's framing, not finance."



Step 9: Test And Refine The Positioning



Testing framework:



Content testing:

Track engagement on guesswork-led posts versus bottleneck-led posts

Monitor save rates on cost-of-inaction content

A/B headlines that feature "intelligence" versus "predictability" versus "bottleneck"

Track which 4-step programme references trigger the most DMs



Sales conversation testing:

Note when prospects nod hardest - that's the line that's working

Track which objections still surface after pre-empt copy is in place

Record sales call openers - "is there a single decision in your business that's actually data-driven?" - and measure responses



Audience feedback collection:

On the Audit call: "what made you book?" - track verbatim answers

After purchase: "what made you commit?" - track verbatim answers

After 30 days: "what's the biggest unlock so far?" - feeds the next round of testimonials and content



Metrics that matter:

Audit call bookings per week

Audit-to-buy conversion rate

Partner-attended-call rate (should be 60%+)

Time-to-first-value after Setup (target: visible AI intelligence within 14 days)

Programme NPS at day 90



Step 10: Voice And Format Standards For All Positioning Content



How positioning copy must sound and look across every surface (ads, sales pages, content, emails, sales scripts):



Voice:

Yorkshire-direct, mate-to-mate

British English throughout

No em-dashes, ever

No "transformation journey", "unlock", "level up", "dive in", "game changer"

Speak to men and women equally

Lead with guesswork and bottleneck



Format:

Short sentences. Punchy.

Specific numbers, not vague claims (£120K, not "huge cost")

Real names, real industries (when used with permission)

Verbatim prospect quotes inserted directly

Programme structure (Audit → Setup → Roadmap → Map) referenced where logical

Partner-inclusive CTA on every page



Bottom line: this offer doesn't need new positioning every quarter. The spine stays the same. Every campaign refines the language a little, the cost-of-inaction stack a little, the proof points a little. But the spine - guesswork to predictable, bottleneck to free, AI tools to AI intelligence, manual to copy-paste, vulnerable to defended - that spine doesn't move.
