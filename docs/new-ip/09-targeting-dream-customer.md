9. Targeting Dream Customer



Dream Customer Targeting Guide

The Business Owner Guessing Growth Without AI Intelligence



Right, so let me tell you exactly who we're going after. Forget the niche-coach-with-a-hybrid-practice nonsense from the last version. That's gone. The person we're going after now is an open business owner who's guessing their way through growth because they've got no AI intelligence behind a single decision they make. Doesn't matter if they're a coach, run an agency, sell a course, or do B2B consulting. Doesn't matter if they're a man or a woman. The pain is identical. They're guessing their content. Guessing their acquisition. Guessing their sales. Every campaign feels like a coin flip. They're doing every single thing themselves on top of that. And they're watching the AI era arrive without a single system to meet it.



That's our person.



1. Demographics



Look, this isn't your typical 25-year-old "guru" market. We're talking 30 to 50, mostly 35 to 50. Old enough to have built something real. Young enough to know they cannot keep grinding like this for another decade.



Men and women equally. The old IP framed this as a male market - that's wrong. Calls show female founders feel this exact pain just as hard, sometimes harder because they're often juggling a business and the household at the same time. Whatever the gender, there's nearly always a partner involved - spouse, business partner, co-founder, whoever. Big decisions go 50/50 with that partner. If you're not getting both of them on the call, you're talking to half a buyer.



Revenue's £100K to £500K a year. The proper hot zone is £8.5K to £30K monthly. Some are stuck under £10K trying to crack it. Others have crossed it and stalled at £20-30K because the systems behind them haven't caught up. That stall is where most of them live.



Where they are: UK heavy. Manchester, Leeds, Birmingham, Sheffield, smaller cities. Then a chunk of internationals - Dubai mostly, some Spain, some Australia. A lot of them are mid-relocation. Fresh start after a burnout, after a bad investment, after a rough year.



The setup: founder-led service businesses. Coaches, fitness folk, health practitioners, agency owners running PR or content or marketing or AI consulting, course and membership operators, B2B consultants, hybrid in-person plus online types. Doesn't matter which. The pain is the same.



The team: solo, or them plus one part-time helper. A VA. A part-time content person. A partner sharing the admin. No proper hires yet. Hiring feels expensive, risky, slow. So they don't.



The education: most of them are well-read. Uni-educated or self-taught proper specialists in their thing. They've already paid for 5+ programmes. They've got at least one regret. Some of them have got 13.

3. Pain Points



What they're typing into Google late at night:

"How to stop guessing what to post"

"How to get consistent leads as a solo business owner"

"How to take time off without losing income"



Now here's the deeper stuff they don't say out loud, but it's eating them alive:



The Guesswork Tax. This is the hero pain. Every single decision they make - what to post, who to chase, what to charge, where to spend, when to launch - is guesswork. No data behind it. No predictability. No AI intelligence telling them what's actually working. Content's a guess. Acquisition's a guess. Sales is a guess. Every campaign's a coin flip and they've run out of patience for it. They want to KNOW what's working before they spend the time, the money, the energy. They want intelligence, not more output.



The Bottleneck Identity Crisis. The whole business runs through them. Every decision, every output, every client touch. They know it's mental but they cannot see a way out without dropping balls. The line that lands hardest on every sales call: "you are the bottleneck of your business." It cracks something open every single time. The reason they're the bottleneck is they're the only one with all the context - because nothing's documented, nothing's intelligent, nothing's systemised.



The Trust Trauma. This is the big one nobody talks about. They've been "promised the world" by mentors who vanished the second the payment cleared. Some of them have got 13 mentors behind them. Some have got debt because of those mentors. When they object on price, they don't actually mean price. They mean "I don't trust you yet, prove you're not the same as the last 13."



The Partner Pressure. Every big decision is 50/50 with their partner. The partner's often overwhelmed too, or doesn't believe in the new direction, or just isn't on the call. This is the silent block on most "I'll get back to you" answers. Get the partner in the room.



The Vicious Cycle. They cannot grow without systems. They've got no time to build systems because they're busy growing. They cannot afford to slow down to fix it because cash flow depends on staying busy. Round and round.



The AI Era Anxiety. They're watching competitors using AI to scale faster, post more, win more. They don't know what's actually working versus what's hype. They're scared of being left behind AND scared of investing in the wrong stack. So they freeze.



The Plate Anxiety. "I cannot add any more stress to my plate." Every new opportunity, every new investment, gets filtered through this fear first. It's the reason most "yes" decisions stall. Got to flip this - the offer LIGHTENS the plate, doesn't add to it.



4. Core Desires & Aspirations



Right, so what they actually say they want:

"Predictability in my numbers, my pipeline, my month."

"Comfortably making £10K a month, consistently."

"Move from £10K to £20-30K monthly."

"A business I could sell, exit, or be a silent partner in."

"Take two weeks off without the business falling over."

"Get my time back so my kids see me thriving instead of breaking."

"Stop being scared every time a client leaves."



But what they're really chasing, deeper down:



Predictability. Above everything else. Knowing what next week looks like. Knowing what next month brings. Knowing what's actually working before they invest more in it. AI intelligence behind every decision instead of guesswork in front of it. This is the headline desire and the offer needs to lead with it.



Freedom. Time freedom first. Financial freedom second. The order matters. Every single one of these owners will pick "two weeks off without panicking" over "another £5K month" if you make them choose.



Family Validation. They want their kids and their partner to see them as the steady, capable provider. Not the stressed, exhausted shell they actually are right now. "I want my kids to see their dad thriving" - that's verbatim from a call. Mums on calls say the same about their kids seeing a present mum. Use the language directly.



Trust In Themselves Again. After a string of bad decisions and burns, they need to prove to themselves they made the RIGHT call this time.



Permission To Stop Doing Everything. They've been raised on "if you want it done right, do it yourself" and it's killing them. They need permission to delegate, automate, step back. Without that permission, they keep grinding.



Authority In The AI Era. They want to be the person in their industry who's clearly cracked AI. Not the one scrambling to keep up. Personal brand plus AI adoption - that's the moat.



5. Buying Triggers



What gets a yes out of them:



Done-with-you delivery. Not another course. Not another framework. Hand-holding. Copy-paste assets. Someone walking next to them through the build. They've sat through enough video modules.



A specific, concrete promise. "Replace £100K+/month in staff wages" lands. It's specific. "10x your output" is hype noise to them now, they've heard it a hundred times.



Predictability and AI intelligence framing. The second the conversation moves from "AI tools" to "AI intelligence - the system that tells you what's working, what's not, and what to do next" - that's when they lean forward. They want to stop guessing. Lead with that.



The bottleneck reflection. Saying "you are the bottleneck of your business" out loud and watching them nod. Every single time.



Personal brand plus AI adoption framed together. They've heard each one separately a hundred times. Hearing them together as the only real defence in the AI era - that's fresh.



Real proof. Not vague "transformation testimonials". Real before-and-after of the same operator. Specific numbers. Real industries.



What they push back with, and how to handle it:



"I cannot add more to my plate."

Bypass: "Mate, that's exactly why we build the systems FOR you and hand them over as copy-paste SOPs. Your plate gets lighter, not heavier."



"I've been burnt by mentors before."

Bypass: "I get it. Most programmes hand you videos and a Slack channel and disappear. We do the implementation alongside you across 90 days through the Growth Intelligence Audit, the Claude AI Specialist Setup, the Personal Brand and AI Adoption Roadmap, and the 90-Day Growth Map. Built for you, used by you, owned by you."



"My partner needs to be on board."

Bypass: "Bring them onto the next call. Most decisions like this stall because the partner isn't in the conversation. Let's walk through it together so you both see the same picture."



"I cannot afford it right now."

Bypass: "Stack it against the cost of staying manual for another twelve months. Roughly £120K of hidden cost across lost time, lost content, lost revenue, lost peace of mind. Then ask the question again."



"What if AI changes again next year?"

Bypass: "We build adaptive systems on Claude that evolve with the model, not fragile setups dependent on one tool. The methodology outlives the tool."



6. Decision-Making Patterns



Look, these aren't impulse buyers. These are cautious veterans who've already lost money. They've already done the research. They've followed Ben for weeks or months. The decision usually hinges on one clarifying call where they go "right, this one's different."



Research phase: 1 to 3 weeks usually, sometimes longer.



What they're weighing on the call:

Is this proper done-with-you, or is it dressed-up done-by-yourself?

Is the person on the call honest about what's in and what's not?

Will this actually give them the AI intelligence they need to stop guessing?

Will the partner agree on the next call?

Is the cost-of-inaction frame real or made up?

Does this person actually sound like they've been through it, or is it another polished pitch?



What pushes them to act now:

A recent month where they undercharged or overworked

A client win they cannot replicate

A peer posting a case study using AI properly

A specific pressure point - a holiday they cannot take, a kid milestone they missed, a partner running on empty

9. Barriers To Change



What's blocking them, and what to do about each one:



The Guesswork Comfort Zone. Strange one - guessing is uncomfortable but at least it's familiar. Switching to data and intelligence requires admitting most of the last decade was guesswork. That's an ego cost. Counter: reframe it as "you've built this far on instinct, imagine what happens when you add intelligence behind the instinct."



The Plate Anxiety. Every new commitment gets filtered through "can I cope with this on top of everything else?". Answer's almost always no, even when the new thing is the thing that would lighten the plate. Counter: reframe the offer as plate-removal, not plate-addition. The first deliverable is an Audit that tells them what to STOP doing.



The Trust Trauma. Past mentors who vanished. Frameworks that all looked the same. Debt left behind. Programmes that overpromised and under-delivered. Counter: be the literal opposite of every operator who burnt them. Done-with-you. Specific deliverables. Named programme structure. Promises kept fully.



The Partner Pressure. Partner's overwhelmed or sceptical or just not in the conversation. Decisions stall. Counter: invite the partner onto the second call by default. Address them directly. Give them the language to say yes alongside the operator.



The Identity Mismatch. "I'm the kind of person who builds it themselves" sits in conflict with "I need someone to do this with me". Counter: reframe done-with-you as the highest-leverage, most ownership-friendly path. They still own the systems at the end. Nothing taken away.



The AI Era Paralysis. They know AI is the answer. They cannot tell which tools, which workflows, which approach. They freeze. Counter: one stack. Claude Specialist. Specific roadmap. Specific 90-Day Growth Map. Decisions made for them where decision fatigue blocks progress.



What it takes to actually build trust:

Real screenshots of real AI intelligence systems

Specific named programme structure (Audit, Setup, Roadmap, 90-Day Map)

Honest pricing conversation, with the cost-of-inaction frame

A second call with the partner included

A founder-style story from Ben about being burnt and rebuilding

No corporate language. No "journey". No hype.



10. Messaging Strategy



The tone that lands:



Direct but not cold. Speak as a peer who's been there. Acknowledge the trust trauma without dwelling on it. Lead with the guesswork reflection, then the bottleneck reflection.



Practical with vision. Specific deliverables this quarter. Real freedom in twelve months. Both, not one or the other.



Confident without hype. Specific numbers. Named programme. Real screenshots. No "transformation journey". No "unlocking potential". That language gets them rolling their eyes by paragraph two.



Yorkshire-direct, mate-to-mate, with warmth. Plain-speaking. Mate talking to mate where the platform allows it. Never corporate.



The stories that connect:



The founder crash story. Ben rebuilt from a 2018 collapse. That's the proof point that lands hardest with prospects who've been through their own crash. Tell it.



The guesswork reflection. A short story showing exactly what running on guesswork looks like, then naming it for them. Followed by what AI intelligence actually is and how it replaces every guess with a known.



The bottleneck reflection. A short story showing exactly what running the bottlenecked life looks like, then naming it for them.



The partner conversation. Stories that include the partner and the family. Decisions in real businesses are 50/50, content has to reflect that.



The kid moment. "I want my kids to see their parent thriving." Use this directly in content, in emails, in carousels. It's gold.



The AI era frame. "Two things separate winners right now: Personal Brand and AI Adoption." Twin pillars. Repeat it across everything.

Real screenshots of Claude AI Specialist setups producing actual intelligence

Specific revenue numbers and time savings (not vague percentages)

Named clients with permission, real industries
