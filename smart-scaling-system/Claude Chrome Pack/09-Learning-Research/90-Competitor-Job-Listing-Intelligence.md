# Prompt 90: Competitor Job Listing Intelligence

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Competitor job posting page
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Extracts strategic intelligence from a competitor's job listings. Job posts reveal what a company is building, which tools they use, how their team is structured, and where they're investing - information they'd never put in a press release but freely publish on a careers page.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Competitor Job Listing Intelligence

Analyse this job listing and extract strategic intelligence about the company.

## My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- This competitor's name: [COMPETITOR NAME]
- What I compete with them on: [e.g. same audience, same service, same market]

## Extract This Intelligence
1. What they're building - what does this hire tell us about their roadmap
2. Team structure signals - what does this role's reporting line reveal
3. Growth indicators - are they scaling, pivoting, or filling a gap
4. Tech stack and tools - what software and platforms do they use
5. Budget signals - what does the salary range tell us about their investment level
6. Skills they value - what capabilities are they prioritising right now
7. Weaknesses exposed - what gaps does this hiring need reveal
8. Strategic opportunities for me - how can I use this intelligence
9. Content angles - what can I create based on what this reveals about the market

Tell me what this job post reveals that the company probably didn't intend to share.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A breakdown of what this hire signals about the competitor's strategic direction
- Insights into their team structure, tools, and investment priorities
- Weaknesses and gaps this job listing accidentally reveals
- Specific opportunities you can exploit based on what you've learned
- Content angles that position you ahead of where they're heading
- A clear picture of market trends based on what competitors are hiring for

## Pro Tips
- Check competitor job listings monthly - it's one of the most underused sources of competitive intelligence available
- Look for patterns across multiple listings from the same company to understand their full strategic direction
- When they're hiring for something you already offer as a service, that's a direct sales opportunity - reach out to companies posting those roles
