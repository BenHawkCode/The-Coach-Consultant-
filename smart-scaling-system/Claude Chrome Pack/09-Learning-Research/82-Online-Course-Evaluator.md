# Prompt 82: Online Course Evaluator

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Course sales page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Evaluates an online course before you spend money on it. Analyses the sales page, curriculum, pricing, and positioning to give you an honest assessment of whether it's worth buying - and what's likely missing that the sales page won't tell you.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Online Course Evaluator

Analyse this course sales page and give me an honest evaluation before I buy.

## My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My current skill level in this topic: [BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED]
- My specific goal from taking this course: [e.g. learn paid ads, build a funnel, improve copywriting]
- My budget sensitivity: [e.g. under £500 is fine / need strong ROI justification for £1000+]

## Evaluate These Areas
1. Curriculum breakdown - what's actually covered vs what's implied
2. Value vs price assessment - is this priced fairly for what's included
3. What's likely MISSING from the curriculum based on the topic
4. Red flags in the sales copy (fake scarcity, vague outcomes, inflated claims)
5. Who this course is actually best suited for - and whether that's me
6. Alternative ways to learn this same material (free or cheaper options)
7. Questions I should ask the creator before purchasing

Be brutally honest. I'd rather save my money than waste it on hype.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A breakdown of what the course actually teaches vs what the sales page implies
- An honest value-for-money assessment based on the pricing and content depth
- A list of topics the course likely skips that you'll need to learn elsewhere
- Red flags in the marketing that signal potential overpricing or underdelivery
- A clear recommendation: buy, skip, or find an alternative

## Pro Tips
- Use this on courses priced above £200 where the purchase decision actually matters to your budget
- Run it on your own course sales page to see what objections potential buyers might have
- Compare the output against two or three competing courses to find the best fit
