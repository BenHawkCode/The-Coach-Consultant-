# Prompt 83: SaaS Tool Comparison

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Software pricing/features page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Analyses a SaaS tool's features and pricing page to help you decide whether it's the right fit for your business. Breaks down feature gaps, hidden costs, and whether you're paying for bloat you'll never use - so you can make a confident decision without a 14-day trial you'll forget to cancel.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# SaaS Tool Comparison & Assessment

Analyse this software's pricing and features page for my business.

## My Business Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- Business size: [SOLO / SMALL TEAM / 5-10 STAFF]
- Monthly budget for this tool category: [e.g. £50-100/month]
- What I need this tool to do: [e.g. email marketing, CRM, scheduling, project management]
- Tools I currently use: [LIST CURRENT TOOLS]

## Analyse These Areas
1. Feature breakdown - what do I actually get at each pricing tier
2. Which tier is the best fit for my business size and needs
3. Hidden costs - onboarding fees, per-user charges, add-ons, overage fees
4. Feature gaps - what's missing that I'd expect from this category of tool
5. Integration compatibility with my current tools
6. Lock-in risk - how easy is it to migrate away if needed
7. Compare value against common alternatives in this category
8. Give me a clear recommendation: subscribe, trial first, or skip

Be specific about what I'd actually use vs what I'd be paying for and never touching.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A tier-by-tier breakdown showing exactly what you get at each price point
- A recommendation on which specific plan fits your business size
- Hidden costs and gotchas the pricing page buries in fine print
- Feature gaps compared to what competitors typically offer
- A clear verdict on whether to subscribe, trial, or look elsewhere

## Pro Tips
- Run this on two or three competing tools and compare the outputs side by side for a proper evaluation
- Pay close attention to the lock-in risk section - switching costs are the most expensive hidden fee in SaaS
- Use this before any annual billing commitment where you'd be locked in for 12 months
