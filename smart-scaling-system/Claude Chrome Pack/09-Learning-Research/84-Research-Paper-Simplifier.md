# Prompt 84: Research Paper Simplifier

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Academic paper or report in browser
**ROI Level:** Low | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Takes a complex research paper or industry report and translates it into plain English you can actually use. Pulls out the key findings, explains what they mean for your business, and shows you how to reference the data in your content without sounding like a textbook.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Research Paper Simplifier

Break down this research paper or report into plain English I can use.

## My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- How I plan to use this: [e.g. content creation, client presentations, strategic decisions]

## What I Need
1. One-paragraph summary a 15-year-old could understand
2. The 3-5 key findings that actually matter - skip methodology details
3. What these findings mean for business owners in [YOUR NICHE]
4. Any limitations or caveats I should know before citing this
5. Sample size and credibility check - is this study worth referencing
6. 3 ways I could use these findings in my content or marketing
7. A ready-to-use citation line I can drop into social posts or emails

Write it conversationally. No academic jargon.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A plain-English summary that strips out the academic waffle
- The key findings distilled into bullet points you can scan in 30 seconds
- Business implications specific to your niche and audience
- A credibility assessment so you know whether it's worth citing
- Ready-to-use content angles and a citation line for your posts

## Pro Tips
- Use this to build authority content - citing real research separates you from competitors who just share opinions
- Run it on industry reports from McKinsey, HubSpot, or Statista to pull stats for your carousels and emails
- Save the citation lines in a swipe file so you always have data-backed hooks ready to go
