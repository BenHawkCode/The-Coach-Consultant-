# Prompt 89: Grant & Funding Opportunity Analyser

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Grant/funding listing page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Analyses a grant or funding listing and tells you whether it's worth applying for. Checks your eligibility, breaks down the requirements, flags potential dealbreakers, and gives you tips for a stronger application - so you don't waste hours on funding you were never going to get.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Grant & Funding Opportunity Analyser

Analyse this grant or funding listing and tell me if it's worth pursuing.

## My Business Details
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- Business type: [e.g. sole trader, limited company, partnership]
- Location: [YOUR REGION/COUNTRY]
- Business age: [e.g. 2 years trading]
- Annual revenue: [e.g. £80K]
- Team size: [e.g. solo / 3 staff]
- What I'd use the funding for: [e.g. marketing, hiring, tech, product development]

## What I Need
1. Eligibility check - do I qualify based on my business details
2. Key requirements and criteria broken down in plain English
3. Application deadline and timeline
4. What they're actually looking for in successful applicants
5. Red flags or dealbreakers that might disqualify me
6. Tips for making my application stronger
7. Estimated time investment to complete the application
8. Clear verdict: apply, skip, or bookmark for later

Be honest about my chances. Don't let me waste time on a long shot.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A clear eligibility assessment based on your business details
- Requirements translated from bureaucratic language into plain English
- The deadline and key dates you need to hit
- Specific tips on what to emphasise in your application
- An honest assessment of your chances and whether the time investment is worth it
- A clear apply/skip/bookmark recommendation

## Pro Tips
- Run this on multiple grants in one session to quickly filter down to the ones actually worth your time
- Pay close attention to the "what they're looking for" section - most grants fund specific outcomes, not general business growth
- If the verdict is "apply", copy the tips section into a separate doc and use it as your application framework
