# Prompt 85: Legal Page Translator

**Category:** Learning & Research
**Where to Use:** Any terms/privacy page
**ROI Level:** Low | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Translates terms of service, privacy policies, and legal pages into plain English so you actually understand what you're agreeing to. Highlights red flags, data usage practices, and key obligations buried in the legalese - before you click "I agree" on autopilot.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Legal Page Translator

Translate this terms of service or privacy policy into plain English.

## My Context
- I'm a: [BUSINESS OWNER / CUSTOMER / BOTH]
- The tool or platform this relates to: [NAME OF PLATFORM]
- My main concern: [e.g. data ownership, cancellation terms, liability, content rights]

## What I Need
1. A plain-English summary of what this agreement actually says
2. My key obligations - what am I agreeing to do (or not do)
3. Their key obligations - what are they promising (or not promising)
4. Data usage breakdown - what data they collect, store, share, and sell
5. Content ownership - who owns what I create or upload on their platform
6. Cancellation and refund terms - what happens when I want to leave
7. Red flags - anything unusual, aggressive, or worth pushing back on
8. Comparison to industry standard - is this normal or overreaching

Highlight anything I should genuinely be concerned about.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A clear summary of the full agreement in language you can actually understand
- Your obligations and rights laid out in simple bullet points
- A data usage breakdown showing exactly what happens to your information
- Red flags highlighted with an explanation of why they matter
- A verdict on whether the terms are standard or worth questioning

## Pro Tips
- Run this before signing up for any SaaS tool that will store your client data - you're responsible for that data under GDPR
- Use it on your own terms of service to check whether they actually protect you the way you think they do
- Pay extra attention to the content ownership section if you're uploading proprietary frameworks or course material
