# Prompt 61: Prospect Research Before Call

**Category:** Sales & Conversion
**Where to Use:** Prospect's website
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Builds a complete research dossier on a prospect before your sales call. Pulls their business model, likely challenges, team size, recent activity, and generates personalised talking points so you walk into every call prepared and credible.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Pre-Call Prospect Research

Analyse this website and build me a complete prospect research brief for an upcoming sales call.

## Business Overview
- What does this company do? Summarise their core offer in 1-2 sentences
- What business model are they running? (service, product, SaaS, hybrid)
- Who is their target audience based on messaging and positioning?
- Estimate their company size (team page, about page, footer clues)

## Challenges & Pain Points
- Based on their positioning and [YOUR NICHE], what are their 3 most likely challenges right now?
- What gaps exist in their current setup that [YOUR OFFER] could solve?
- Are there any visible signs of struggle (outdated content, broken links, missing pages)?

## Recent Activity & News
- Note any recent blog posts, case studies, press mentions, or announcements
- Flag any hiring signals, new product launches, or expansion indicators
- Identify their most recent content and what it tells us about their current priorities

## Personalised Talking Points
- Give me 5 specific conversation starters based on what you found
- Suggest 3 pain-point questions I can ask that will feel relevant to their situation
- Identify 2-3 compliments or observations I can open with to build rapport

## Pitch Angle
- Based on everything above, recommend the strongest angle to position [YOUR OFFER]
- What specific outcome would resonate most with this prospect?
- What objections should I prepare for based on their current setup?

Format as a scannable brief I can review in 5 minutes before the call.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A concise business overview including model, audience, and estimated size
- Their most likely pain points mapped to your specific offer
- Recent activity and signals that reveal current priorities
- Ready-to-use conversation starters and rapport builders
- A recommended pitch angle tailored to their situation
- Objection preparation based on visible evidence

## Pro Tips
- Run this 30 minutes before your call and keep it open on a second screen for reference
- Combine with their LinkedIn profile (use Prompt 62) for a complete picture of both the company and the decision maker
- Save each research brief in a client folder so you can reference it in follow-up calls and proposals
