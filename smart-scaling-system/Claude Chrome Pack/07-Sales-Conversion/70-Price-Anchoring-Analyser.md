# Prompt 70: Price Anchoring Analyser

**Category:** Sales & Conversion
**Where to Use:** Competitor pricing page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Decodes how a competitor uses price anchoring, tier structure, decoy pricing, and value framing to influence buying decisions. Gives you specific recommendations for applying the same psychological principles to your own pricing page.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Price Anchoring & Pricing Psychology Analysis

Analyse this pricing page and decode every pricing psychology tactic being used. I sell [YOUR OFFER] to [YOUR AUDIENCE] in [YOUR NICHE].

## Tier Structure
- How many pricing tiers are displayed and what are they?
- Which tier is positioned as the "recommended" or "most popular" option?
- What is the price gap between each tier and is it strategic?
- How do the feature lists differ between tiers - what is included vs excluded?

## Anchoring Tactics
- What is the highest price shown and how is it being used as an anchor?
- Are there any "was/now" or crossed-out prices creating a deal perception?
- Is there a comparison to external costs (e.g. "less than your daily coffee")?
- How is the value framed relative to the price (ROI statements, cost-per-day)?

## Decoy Pricing
- Is there a tier that exists primarily to make another tier look better?
- Which tier are they actually trying to sell the most of?
- How does the decoy tier influence the perceived value of the target tier?
- Would removing any tier change how the others are perceived?

## Value Framing & Comparison
- How do they justify the price (results, time saved, revenue generated)?
- Are there any "what you would pay elsewhere" comparisons?
- Do they break the price down into smaller units (per month, per day, per session)?
- What bonuses or added value are used to increase perceived worth?

## Psychological Triggers
- What urgency or scarcity elements are used on the pricing page?
- Is there social proof positioned near the pricing (testimonials, user counts)?
- What risk-reversal is offered (guarantees, free trials, refund policies)?
- Are there any commitment-reducing elements (cancel anytime, no contract)?

## Recommendations for [YOUR BRAND]
- Which tactics from this page would work for [YOUR OFFER]?
- Suggest a 3-tier pricing structure based on what works here
- Recommend 3 specific anchoring techniques to apply to your own pricing
- Identify which tactics to avoid because they would not suit [YOUR AUDIENCE]

Include a summary table comparing their approach to what you recommend for my business.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A complete breakdown of their tier structure and the psychology behind it
- Every anchoring tactic identified and explained
- Decoy pricing analysis showing which tier they actually want you to buy
- Value framing techniques you can adapt for your own pricing
- Psychological triggers and risk-reversal elements decoded
- A tailored pricing strategy recommendation for your business

## Pro Tips
- Run this on 5 competitors in your niche and create a spreadsheet comparing their pricing structures - patterns will emerge that reveal what the market expects
- The most effective pricing pages anchor high first then reveal the "real" price - make sure your highest tier is visible before your target tier
- Never copy a competitor's exact pricing structure without understanding why it works - what converts for their audience may not convert for yours
