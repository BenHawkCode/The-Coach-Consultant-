# Prompt 69: Case Study Builder From Client Site

**Category:** Sales & Conversion
**Where to Use:** Client's website or testimonial page
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Medium

## What This Does
Builds a publishable case study draft by extracting transformation signals, results, and testimonial content from a client's website. Turns scattered proof points into a structured before-and-after narrative you can use in proposals, sales pages, and content.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Case Study Builder

Analyse this website and extract everything needed to build a compelling case study. This is a client of [YOUR BRAND] in [YOUR NICHE] and I want to showcase the results of working together.

## Client Background
- What does this client's business do and who do they serve?
- What stage are they at now based on their website (startup, growth, established)?
- What is their positioning and how do they present themselves?

## Before Signals
- Based on the website, what problems were they likely facing before working with you?
- What gaps or weaknesses can you infer from their current or previous setup?
- What was their probable situation regarding [YOUR OFFER] before your involvement?
- Extract any "origin story" or "about" content that hints at past struggles

## After Signals & Results
- What visible results or achievements are showcased on their site?
- Pull any specific numbers, metrics, or quantifiable outcomes mentioned
- Note any awards, features, client logos, or social proof that indicate growth
- What does their current quality of work suggest about their transformation?

## Testimonial & Quote Extraction
- Find any testimonials, reviews, or client quotes on the site
- Extract quotes that describe transformation, results, or experience
- Note any video testimonials or social proof that could be referenced
- Identify the strongest single quote that captures the overall result

## Case Study Draft
- Write a compelling headline that leads with the result
- Structure the narrative: Challenge > Solution > Result
- Include 3-5 key metrics or outcomes (use what is available, flag what to confirm with the client)
- Write a 2-3 sentence summary suitable for a sales page or proposal
- Draft 3 pull quotes formatted for use in marketing materials

## Publishing Recommendations
- Suggest 5 places this case study could be used (sales page, email, social, proposal, ads)
- Recommend the best format for each placement (full story, summary, single stat, quote card)
- Flag any claims that need client approval before publishing
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A complete client background and business context
- Before-and-after narrative built from visible website signals
- Extracted testimonials and quotes ready for marketing use
- A structured case study draft with headline, metrics, and summary
- Pull quotes formatted for sales pages and proposals
- Publishing recommendations across multiple channels

## Pro Tips
- Always send the draft to your client for approval and ask them to fill in the specific numbers you flagged - they will usually add even better data than you expected
- The most powerful case studies focus on one specific transformation rather than trying to cover everything you did together
- Create a short version (3 sentences with one stat) and a long version (full narrative) - you will use the short version ten times more often
