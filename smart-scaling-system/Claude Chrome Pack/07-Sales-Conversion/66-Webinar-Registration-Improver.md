# Prompt 66: Webinar Registration Improver

**Category:** Sales & Conversion
**Where to Use:** Your webinar registration page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Audits your webinar registration page for weak headlines, missing urgency elements, poor social proof placement, and form friction. Gives you specific copy and layout improvements to increase your registration rate.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Webinar Registration Page Audit

Analyse this webinar registration page and give me specific improvements to increase sign-ups. My brand is [YOUR BRAND] and this targets [YOUR AUDIENCE] in [YOUR NICHE].

## Headline Effectiveness
- Does the headline communicate a specific outcome or transformation?
- Is it clear what the attendee will walk away with?
- Rewrite the headline in 3 stronger variations that lead with the result
- Rate the subheadline - does it support the main promise or waste space?

## Benefit Bullets
- Are the current bullets outcome-focused or feature-focused?
- Do they address the specific pain points of [YOUR AUDIENCE]?
- Rewrite the bullet points to be sharper and more specific
- Suggest 3 additional bullets that would increase perceived value

## Urgency & Scarcity
- What urgency elements are currently on the page (countdown, limited spots, date)?
- What is missing that would create genuine urgency to register now?
- Suggest 3 urgency elements that feel authentic rather than manipulative
- Is the date, time, and duration clearly communicated?

## Trust & Social Proof
- What social proof is visible (attendee count, testimonials, credentials)?
- Where should social proof be placed for maximum impact?
- Suggest 3 trust elements that would reduce registration hesitation
- Is the host's credibility established clearly?

## Form & Technical Friction
- How many fields does the registration form have? Which can be removed?
- Is the submit button text specific ("Reserve My Spot") or generic ("Submit")?
- Does the page load quickly and work properly on mobile?
- Is there a clear privacy statement near the form?

## Specific Improvements
- List 8 changes ranked by likely impact on registration rate
- Rewrite the call-to-action button text
- Suggest an above-the-fold layout that would convert better
- Recommend one element to add that most registration pages miss
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Three stronger headline variations you can test immediately
- Rewritten benefit bullets that focus on outcomes
- Urgency elements that feel authentic and drive action
- Social proof recommendations with specific placement suggestions
- Form friction fixes to reduce drop-off
- Eight prioritised improvements ranked by impact

## Pro Tips
- The single biggest registration killer is a vague headline - if people cannot tell exactly what they will learn in 5 seconds, they leave
- Add a "What You Will Learn" section with exactly 3 bullet points - odd numbers and specificity outperform vague benefit lists
- Test your page on mobile before anything else - over 60% of registrations happen on phones
