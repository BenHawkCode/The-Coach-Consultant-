# Prompt 63: Personalised Proposal From Website

**Category:** Sales & Conversion
**Where to Use:** Prospect's website
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Medium

## What This Does
Generates a personalised proposal outline by reverse-engineering a prospect's website to identify their problems, gaps, and opportunities. Maps your solution directly to their situation so the proposal feels custom-built rather than templated.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Personalised Proposal Generator

Analyse this website and create a tailored proposal outline for offering [YOUR OFFER] to this business. My brand is [YOUR BRAND] and I serve [YOUR AUDIENCE] in [YOUR NICHE].

## Their Current Situation
- Summarise what this business does and who they serve
- Identify 3-5 specific problems or gaps visible from their website
- Note what they are doing well that we should acknowledge
- Flag any missed opportunities that are costing them results

## Proposed Solution
- Map each identified problem directly to a specific element of [YOUR OFFER]
- Explain the fix in plain language (no jargon, no fluff)
- For each solution element, describe the tangible outcome they will get

## Specific Deliverables
- List 5-8 concrete deliverables tailored to what this business actually needs
- For each deliverable, include a one-line description of what it includes
- Order them by impact (highest value first)

## Projected Outcomes
- Based on the gaps identified, project 3-5 realistic outcomes they could expect
- Frame outcomes in terms of revenue, time saved, or growth metrics
- Include a timeline estimate for when they would start seeing results

## Pricing Frame
- Suggest how to anchor the price against the cost of their current problems
- Recommend a value-based framing statement (not just a number)
- Include a comparison: cost of doing nothing vs cost of working with [YOUR BRAND]

## Proposal Structure
- Write a 3-sentence opening paragraph that references their specific situation
- Outline the full proposal in the order it should be presented
- End with a clear next step and call to action

Format this as a proposal outline I can flesh out and send within 30 minutes.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A situation analysis that proves you have done your homework
- Problems mapped directly to your solution with clear deliverables
- Projected outcomes framed around their specific business
- A pricing framework that anchors value before presenting cost
- A ready-to-complete proposal structure with personalised opening
- A clear next step that moves the deal forward

## Pro Tips
- Run Prompt 61 (Prospect Research) first, then use this for the proposal - the combination is significantly more powerful than either alone
- Always edit the projected outcomes to be conservative rather than optimistic - underpromise and overdeliver builds trust
- Send the proposal within 24 hours of the discovery call while the conversation is still fresh in their mind
