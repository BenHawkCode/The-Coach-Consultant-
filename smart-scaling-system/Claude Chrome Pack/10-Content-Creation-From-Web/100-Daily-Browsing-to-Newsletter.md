# Prompt 100: Daily Browsing to Newsletter

**Category:** Content Creation from the Web
**Where to Use:** Any collection of articles (run on each tab)
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Medium

## What This Does
Turns your daily browsing into a curated newsletter edition. Run this on each article tab to summarise it, add your take, then compile everything into a structured newsletter with sections, a subject line, and your personality throughout. Builds a newsletter habit without starting from a blank page.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Turn This Article Into a Newsletter Section

Read this article and create a newsletter section I can compile into a weekly edition.

## My Details
- Brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- Niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- Newsletter audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]

## For This Article, Create

### Summary (2-3 sentences)
- What is the article about and why should my audience care?
- Focus on the practical takeaway, not the theory

### My Take (2-3 sentences)
- My opinion, reaction, or insight on this topic
- Write in first person - direct, opinionated, conversational
- Connect it to something my audience is dealing with right now

### Key Quote or Stat
- Pull one line from the article worth highlighting
- Include the source attribution

### Category Tag
- Label this section: [Industry News / Tool of the Week / Strategy Insight / Trend Watch / Hot Take]

---

## When I've Run This on All Articles, Use This Final Prompt:

# Compile My Newsletter Edition

Take all the sections I've created and build a complete newsletter.

## Newsletter Structure
1. Subject line (under 50 characters, curiosity-driven)
2. Preview text (under 90 characters)
3. Opening paragraph - personal, sets the tone for this week's theme
4. 3-5 curated sections (from the articles above)
5. One "Quick Hits" section - 2-3 one-liner links I found interesting
6. Closing CTA - soft sell to [YOUR OFFER] or a question for replies
7. Sign-off

## Rules
- The newsletter should feel curated, not aggregated
- My voice throughout - not a news roundup robot
- Total read time: under 5 minutes
- Every section must have my perspective, not just a summary
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Individual newsletter sections for each article you browse
- Each section with summary, your take, and a key quote
- A compiled newsletter with subject line, opening, curated sections, and CTA
- Category tags for consistent newsletter structure
- A complete edition ready to paste into your email platform

## Pro Tips
- Keep a running tab of 5-7 articles throughout the week, then batch this on Friday
- The "My Take" section is what separates a good newsletter from a link dump - never skip it
- Track which category tags get the most clicks and weight future editions accordingly
