# Prompt 94: Competitor Content Rewriter

**Category:** Content Creation from the Web
**Where to Use:** Competitor blog post
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Medium

## What This Does
Takes a competitor's blog post and helps you create a stronger version for your own brand. Same topic, different angle, your voice, your examples, your CTA. Not copying - outperforming. You end up with a piece of content that covers the same ground but positions you as the authority.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Create a Better Version of This Competitor's Content

Read this competitor's blog post and help me create a superior version for my brand.

## My Details
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- My website: [YOUR WEBSITE]

## Analysis First
1. What is the core topic and angle of this post?
2. What does it do well?
3. Where is it weak, vague, or surface-level?
4. What questions does it leave unanswered?
5. What would make someone choose my version over theirs?

## Then Create My Version
- Same core topic but with a stronger, more specific angle
- Open with a hook that's more compelling than theirs
- Add depth where they stayed surface-level
- Include practical examples relevant to my audience
- Write in my voice - direct, conversational, no fluff
- Close with a CTA that drives traffic to [YOUR OFFER]

## Deliverables
- Full blog post (800-1200 words)
- Meta title and description for SEO
- 3 alternative headlines to test
- A social media teaser post to promote it
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A competitor analysis highlighting their content's strengths and gaps
- A full blog post that covers the same topic with more depth and authority
- SEO meta title and description ready to publish
- Three alternative headlines for A/B testing
- A social teaser post to drive traffic to your version

## Pro Tips
- Run this on the top 3 ranking posts for your target keyword to create the definitive piece
- Focus on the gaps they left - that's where you win
- Never publish anything that reads like a rewrite - it should feel like original thought
