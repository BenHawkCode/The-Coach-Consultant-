# Prompt 99: Podcast Guest Bio Prep

**Category:** Content Creation from the Web
**Where to Use:** Podcast host's website or LinkedIn
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Prepares you for a podcast guest appearance by analysing the host's website or LinkedIn profile. Maps their audience, reviews past episodes, identifies talking points that will land, helps you pitch your angle, and writes a suggested intro the host can use. Walk into every podcast fully prepared.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Prepare Me for This Podcast Appearance

Read this podcast host's website/LinkedIn and prepare me for a guest appearance.

## My Details
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- My website: [YOUR WEBSITE]

## Research & Analyse
1. Who is their audience? (demographics, level, pain points)
2. What topics do they typically cover?
3. What are their 3 most popular or recent episodes about?
4. What angle or gap hasn't been covered yet?
5. Where does my expertise overlap with their audience's needs?

## Prepare These Deliverables

### Talking Points (5-7)
- Specific topics I should bring up that serve their audience
- Frame each as a question the host might ask + my ideal answer direction
- Include one contrarian or surprising point to make the episode memorable

### My Pitch Angle
- The single best angle for why I should be on this show
- A one-paragraph pitch I could send to the host
- How my expertise solves a problem their audience has

### Suggested Host Intro (100 words max)
- How the host should introduce me
- Focus on credibility and relevance to their listeners
- No fluff, no generic "entrepreneur and thought leader" language

### Promotion Plan
- 3 social posts to promote the episode before and after it airs
- A CTA I should mention during the episode that drives traffic to [YOUR OFFER]
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A full audience analysis of the podcast and its listeners
- 5-7 tailored talking points with suggested question/answer frameworks
- A pitch angle and one-paragraph outreach message
- A host intro script ready to send
- Three social posts for promoting the episode
- A natural CTA to weave into the conversation

## Pro Tips
- Send the host intro to them before recording - it saves them prep time and ensures you're introduced the way you want
- The contrarian talking point is what makes people share the episode - always have one ready
- Record your own audio of the key insights as backup content for your own channels
