# Prompt 56: Job Board Demand Analyser

**Category:** Market Research & Audience Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Indeed/LinkedIn job search results
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Analyses job listings to identify what the market is paying for right now. Reveals skills in demand, salary trends, which companies are growing, and - most importantly - service opportunities where you can position your offer against the hiring alternative.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Job Board Demand Analysis

Analyse these job listings for market intelligence relevant to [YOUR NICHE].

## Extract the following:

### Skills In Demand
- List every skill, tool, and qualification mentioned across these listings
- Rank them by frequency - which appear in the most listings?
- Note any emerging skills that appear in recent listings but not older ones
- Identify which skills [YOUR AUDIENCE] would benefit from learning or outsourcing

### Salary & Budget Signals
- Note all salary ranges mentioned
- What does this tell us about the budget companies allocate for this function?
- How does this compare to what [YOUR OFFER] costs? (frame the value comparison)
- Identify the price anchor these listings create for your services

### Company Growth Signals
- Which types of companies are hiring for these roles?
- Are they startups, scale-ups, or enterprises?
- What industries are represented?
- What does the volume of listings suggest about market demand?

### Service Opportunities
- For each common role being hired, identify how [YOUR BRAND] could offer the same outcome as a service
- Frame the "hire vs outsource" comparison for [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- Note any listings where the job description matches what you already deliver
- Suggest 3 specific service packages based on recurring job requirements

### Positioning Insights
- What language do companies use to describe the outcomes they want?
- What problems are they trying to solve by hiring?
- How can [YOUR BRAND] use this language in its own marketing?
- What objections might arise when positioning your offer against a full-time hire?

### Content Ideas
- Suggest 5 content pieces based on the demand patterns you see
- Each should position [YOUR BRAND] as a smarter alternative to hiring

Be specific. Use data from the listings, not generalisations.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A ranked list of skills the market is paying for right now
- Salary benchmarks that help you price your services with confidence
- Service package ideas based on what companies are actively hiring for
- Positioning language pulled directly from how businesses describe their needs
- Content ideas that frame your offer as the smarter alternative to a full-time hire
- Growth signals showing which industries and company types are scaling

## Pro Tips
- Search for the role your service replaces (e.g., "social media manager") and use the salary data to anchor your pricing
- The job description requirements section is a ready-made outline for your service page
- Run this monthly to track which skills are rising and falling in demand - this tells you where to invest your own development
