# Prompt 42: Email Dashboard Performance Reader

**Category:** Email & Newsletter Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Your email platform dashboard
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Reads your email marketing dashboard and translates all the numbers into plain English. Instead of staring at open rates and click-throughs wondering what they mean, you get a clear breakdown of what is performing, what is underperforming, and the specific actions to take next.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Email Dashboard Performance Reader

Read this email marketing dashboard and translate everything into plain English. I do not want jargon or generic advice - I want specific, actionable interpretations of my data.

## What I Need
- Performance summary: What is actually performing well and what is underperforming?
- Trend analysis: Are things improving, declining, or flat over the visible time period?
- Benchmarking: How do my metrics compare to standard industry averages for [YOUR NICHE]?
- Red flags: Anything that signals a problem I should address immediately
- Quick wins: The 3 easiest changes I can make to improve results within the next 7 days

## My Details
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- My email frequency: [e.g. weekly / 2x per week / daily]

## Output Format
1. Dashboard health score out of 10 with one-line justification
2. Plain English summary of every visible metric (what it means, whether it is good or bad)
3. Top 3 strengths to double down on
4. Top 3 weaknesses to fix immediately
5. Specific action plan: what to do this week to move the numbers
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- An overall health score with honest justification
- Every metric on screen translated into plain English with context
- Your top strengths identified so you know what to keep doing
- Your top weaknesses ranked by urgency with specific fixes
- A concrete 7-day action plan based on your actual data
- Industry benchmark comparisons for your niche

## Pro Tips
- Run this monthly on the same dashboard to build a performance trend over time
- Screenshot your dashboard before making changes so you can compare after
- Focus on the red flags first - those are costing you the most money right now
