# Prompt 46: Reply Drafter for Client Emails

**Category:** Email & Newsletter Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Client email open in browser (Gmail/Outlook web)
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Drafts a professional reply to a client email that addresses every point raised, maintains the right tone, suggests clear next steps, and keeps things concise. You stop spending 20 minutes crafting replies and start sending polished responses in under 2 minutes.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Client Email Reply Drafter

Read this client email and draft a professional reply. The reply must address every point they raised, maintain a warm but professional tone, and move things forward with clear next steps.

## Reply Requirements
- Address every question or concern raised in their email - do not skip anything
- Match the formality level of the original email (if they are casual, be casual back)
- Keep it concise - no waffle, no filler, no unnecessary pleasantries beyond a brief opener
- Suggest specific next steps with timeframes where appropriate
- If anything in their email is unclear, ask for clarification rather than assuming

## My Details
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My role: [e.g. founder, consultant, account manager]
- My tone preference: [e.g. friendly and direct / formal and detailed / casual and conversational]
- Any context: [e.g. this is a new client / we are mid-project / they have a complaint]

## Output Format
1. Draft reply ready to copy and paste
2. Flag any sensitive points that need careful handling
3. Suggest 2 alternative closing lines if the default does not feel right
4. Note anything in their email I should follow up on separately
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A complete reply draft ready to send with minimal editing
- Every point from their email addressed in a logical order
- Clear next steps with suggested timeframes
- Flags on any sensitive or potentially tricky points
- Alternative closing options for different tones
- Follow-up reminders for anything that needs separate attention

## Pro Tips
- Always read the draft before sending - adjust anything that does not sound like you
- For complaint emails, add a brief acknowledgement of their frustration before jumping to solutions
- Use this for the first draft then personalise with one specific detail only you would know
