# Prompt 45: Email Sequence Auditor

**Category:** Email & Newsletter Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Your email sequence builder view
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Audits your entire email sequence for flow logic, timing gaps, content balance, CTA consistency, and drop-off risk. Instead of wondering why people stop opening or clicking, you get a clear diagnosis of where the sequence breaks down and exactly how to fix it.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Email Sequence Auditor

Audit this email sequence as if you were a conversion rate specialist. I need to know where it is strong, where people are likely dropping off, and what needs fixing.

## Audit These Areas
- Flow logic: Does each email naturally lead to the next? Is there a clear narrative arc?
- Timing & spacing: Are the gaps between emails appropriate? Too fast, too slow, or inconsistent?
- Content balance: What is the ratio of value emails to sales emails? Is the reader getting enough before being asked to buy?
- CTA consistency: Are the calls to action clear, consistent, and building towards the same goal?
- Drop-off risk points: Which specific emails are most likely to cause unsubscribes or disengagement? Why?
- Subject line progression: Do the subject lines maintain curiosity and open-rate momentum throughout?
- Objection handling: Does the sequence address common objections before the main sales push?

## My Details
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- Goal of this sequence: [e.g. sell a course, book discovery calls, nurture leads]

## Output Format
1. Sequence map: visual overview of the flow with email-by-email purpose labels
2. Score each email out of 10 for relevance, engagement potential, and CTA clarity
3. Drop-off risk assessment: flag the 2-3 emails most likely to lose subscribers
4. Content balance chart: value vs sales vs storytelling ratio
5. Specific rewrites or restructures for the weakest emails
6. Suggested additions: any missing emails the sequence needs
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A full sequence map showing the purpose and flow of each email
- Individual scores for every email with specific justifications
- The exact emails where subscribers are most likely to disengage
- Content balance analysis showing your value-to-pitch ratio
- Rewrite suggestions for the weakest emails in the sequence
- Recommendations for missing emails that would strengthen the flow

## Pro Tips
- Run this before launching any new sequence - catching problems before they go live saves subscribers
- Pay closest attention to emails 2-4 in any sequence - that is where most drop-off happens
- If the content balance is heavily skewed towards selling, add a pure value email before each pitch
