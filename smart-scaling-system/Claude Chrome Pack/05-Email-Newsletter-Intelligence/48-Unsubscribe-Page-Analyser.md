# Prompt 48: Unsubscribe Page Analyser

**Category:** Email & Newsletter Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Competitor unsubscribe page
**ROI Level:** Low | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Analyses how competitors handle unsubscribes to identify retention tactics you might be missing. Covers preference centres, downsell options, feedback collection, and win-back copy. Most business owners treat unsubscribe pages as a dead end - the smart ones treat them as a last-chance conversion opportunity.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Unsubscribe Page Analyser

Analyse this unsubscribe page and identify every retention tactic being used. I want to understand how this brand tries to keep subscribers before they leave - and what I should be doing on my own unsubscribe page.

## Analyse These Elements
- Preference centre: Can users adjust frequency or topic preferences instead of fully unsubscribing?
- Downsell options: Are they offering a reduced email frequency as an alternative?
- Feedback collection: Are they asking why the person is leaving? How?
- Win-back copy: What language are they using to persuade people to stay?
- Emotional triggers: Guilt, FOMO, humour, or value reminders?
- Re-engagement offer: Any last-minute incentive to keep the subscriber?
- Design and UX: Is the page designed to make staying easy and leaving hard?
- Confirmation flow: One-click unsubscribe or multi-step?

## My Details
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My current unsubscribe process: [e.g. one-click removal / no preference centre / basic confirmation page]

## Output Format
1. Every retention tactic on this page listed and rated for effectiveness
2. The psychological strategy behind their approach
3. What is missing that could improve retention further
4. A recommended unsubscribe page structure for my brand
5. Example copy for each section of my recommended page
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- A complete list of retention tactics with effectiveness ratings
- The psychology behind their unsubscribe flow explained
- Gaps in their approach that you can improve on
- A full recommended structure for your own unsubscribe page
- Ready-to-use copy for each section of your retention page

## Pro Tips
- Analyse 3-5 competitor unsubscribe pages to build a best-of framework for your own
- Even reducing unsubscribe rates by 10% compounds massively over a year of sending
- A preference centre alone can cut unsubscribes in half compared to a binary stay-or-leave page
