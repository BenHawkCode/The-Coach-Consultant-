# Prompt 26: Ad Longevity Checker

**Category:** Ad Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Meta Ad Library filtered to a single advertiser
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Identifies which ads have been running longest (a strong indicator of profitability), spots seasonal patterns, and flags ads that were killed quickly (meaning they flopped). This separates tested winners from failed experiments - so you only model what actually works.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Ad Longevity & Performance Intelligence

Analyse the start dates and status of every ad visible from this advertiser in the Meta Ad Library. I want to know what's making them money vs what failed.

## Longevity Ranking
List every ad from longest-running to shortest, including:
- Ad start date
- Approximate run time (weeks/months)
- Ad format (image, video, carousel)
- First line of ad copy (the hook)
- Whether it's currently active or inactive
- Offer being promoted

## Evergreen Winners (Running 3+ Months)
For each long-running ad:
- What makes this ad strong enough to keep running?
- What hook pattern does it use?
- What offer is it driving to?
- What creative style is it using?
- Why do you think this one survived when others didn't?

## Quick Deaths (Killed Within 2 Weeks)
For ads that ran briefly and stopped:
- What was the ad trying to do?
- What likely went wrong? (weak hook, wrong audience, unclear offer, poor creative)
- Is there a pattern in what types of ads get killed quickly?

## Timeline Patterns
- Are there seasonal spikes in ad launches? (before events, new year, Q4, etc.)
- Has their creative direction shifted over time?
- Are newer ads following the same patterns as their evergreen winners, or testing new approaches?
- Is there a noticeable increase or decrease in ad volume over time?

## Key Takeaways
Based on longevity data alone, answer:
1. What type of ad consistently works for this advertiser?
2. What type of ad consistently fails for them?
3. What should I model for my own ads in [YOUR NICHE]?
4. What should I avoid based on their failed experiments?

Format the longevity ranking as a table. Put analysis sections below.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Complete longevity ranking table from longest to shortest running
- Evergreen winner breakdown explaining why each successful ad survived
- Failed ad analysis showing what patterns lead to quick kills
- Timeline pattern identification revealing seasonal and strategic shifts
- Clear takeaways on what to model and what to avoid for your own campaigns

## Pro Tips
- An ad running for 6+ months is almost certainly profitable - those are the ones to study most closely and model your own creative after
- Look at the ratio of long-running to short-running ads - a high ratio means this advertiser knows their market well and tests efficiently
- Run this on 3-4 competitors and compare their evergreen winners - the overlapping patterns reveal what the entire market responds to
