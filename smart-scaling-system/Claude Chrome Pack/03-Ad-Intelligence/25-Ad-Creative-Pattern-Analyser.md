# Prompt 25: Ad Creative Pattern Analyser

**Category:** Ad Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Meta Ad Library filtered to a single advertiser
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Identifies the strategic patterns hidden across a single advertiser's ad portfolio - recurring themes, audience segments being targeted, A/B test variations, and which creative approaches they're scaling. This tells you what's actually working for them, not just what they're testing.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Ad Creative Pattern Analysis - Single Advertiser

Analyse all visible ads from this advertiser on the Meta Ad Library. Look beyond individual ads - I want the strategic patterns behind their ad account.

## Creative Format Breakdown
- How many of each format? (static image, video, carousel, collection)
- Which format appears most frequently?
- Is there a clear preferred format they keep returning to?
- Any formats they seem to have tested and abandoned?

## Copy Pattern Analysis
Across all their ads, identify:
- Recurring hooks (first line patterns that keep appearing)
- Common copy structures (long-form story, short punchy, listicle, question-led)
- Phrases or words they use repeatedly (these are likely tested winners)
- How their copy tone shifts between different ads (same voice or multiple angles?)

## Offer Mapping
- How many distinct offers are they promoting?
- List each offer type (lead magnet, webinar, free trial, direct sale, quiz, etc.)
- Which offer appears in the most ads?
- Are they running different offers to different audience segments?

## Audience Segment Signals
Based on the creative variations, identify:
- How many distinct audience segments are they targeting?
- What clues reveal different targeting? (different pain points, different imagery, different language levels)
- Which segment appears to get the most ad variations (indicating it's their primary audience)?

## A/B Testing Patterns
- Identify any ads that are clearly A/B variations of each other
- What elements are they testing? (headlines, images, CTAs, copy length, offers)
- Based on which variations have more active versions, what appears to be winning?

## Scaling Signals
- Which creative themes have the most variations? (more variations = they're scaling what works)
- Which ads have been running longest? (longevity = profitability)
- What creative direction are their newest ads taking? (this shows where they're heading next)

## Strategic Summary
In 3-4 sentences, describe this advertiser's overall ad strategy: who they're targeting, what they're selling, what's working, and what I could learn from them for [YOUR NICHE].
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Format breakdown showing their preferred ad types and what they've abandoned
- Copy pattern analysis revealing their tested hooks and structures
- Complete offer map showing every funnel entry point they're using
- Audience segment identification based on creative variations
- A/B testing insights showing what they're actively optimising
- Scaling signals indicating which creatives are profitable

## Pro Tips
- Focus on advertisers who have 20+ active ads - smaller accounts don't have enough data to reveal meaningful patterns
- Compare the pattern analysis of 2-3 top competitors to find the overlapping strategies (those are market-proven)
- Pay closest attention to the "scaling signals" section - ads with many variations and long run times are the ones making money
