# Prompt 23: Google Ads Spy

**Category:** Ad Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Google search results page with paid ads visible
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Analyses every Google search ad visible for a keyword - extracting headlines, descriptions, extensions, and positioning angles. You get a clear picture of how competitors are bidding, what messaging they're using, and where the gaps are for your own ads.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
# Google Search Ads Intelligence Report

Analyse every paid search ad visible on this Google results page. Extract and organise the following:

## Per-Ad Breakdown
For each ad on the page, capture:
- Display URL / domain
- Headline 1, Headline 2, Headline 3 (each one separately)
- Description lines
- Any ad extensions visible (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, phone numbers, ratings)
- Whether they're using a branded or non-branded approach
- Position on page (top 1, top 2, top 3, bottom)

## Headline Pattern Analysis
- What headline formulas are being used? (benefit-first, keyword-match, question, number-based, brand-led)
- Which headlines mention price or offers?
- Which headlines use urgency or scarcity?
- What's the most common word or phrase across all headlines?

## Positioning Angles
For each advertiser, identify their core positioning:
- Are they competing on price, quality, speed, expertise, or something else?
- What makes their ad different from the others?
- Who appears to be targeting the same audience segment?

## Keyword Targeting Signals
- What is the search term I used?
- Which ads appear to be exact-matching vs broad-matching this keyword?
- What related keywords do the ads suggest competitors are also targeting?

## Gap Analysis
Based on all ads visible, identify:
1. Positioning angles NO ONE is using that could work for [YOUR NICHE]
2. Benefits or features not mentioned by any advertiser
3. The weakest ad on the page and why it's weak
4. How [YOUR BRAND] could differentiate with a stronger ad

Format as a comparison table first, then analysis sections.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Structured table of every ad with headlines, descriptions, and extensions
- Headline formula breakdown showing what copywriting approaches dominate
- Positioning map revealing how each competitor differentiates
- Keyword targeting signals indicating broader campaign strategies
- Gap analysis with specific angles you could use to stand out
- Weakest ad identification showing what to avoid

## Pro Tips
- Search in an incognito window to avoid personalised results skewing the data
- Run the same search with slight keyword variations (e.g. "business coach" vs "business coaching" vs "business coach near me") - you'll often see completely different advertisers and angles
- Screenshot the results before running the prompt, as ads rotate frequently and you want a record of what you analysed
