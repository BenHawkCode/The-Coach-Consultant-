# Prompt 02: Reverse-Engineer Their Sales Page

**Category:** Competitor Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Competitor's sales or landing page
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Breaks down a competitor's entire sales page structure into its component parts - headline formulas, pain/desire framework, objection handling, offer stack, guarantee, and CTA strategy. Gives you a proven blueprint you can adapt for your own sales pages without copying their work.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
Reverse-engineer this entire sales page. Break down every persuasion element, structural decision, and conversion mechanism on this page.

# My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- My target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]

# Full Sales Page Breakdown

## 1. Headline & Hook
- What is the main headline? What formula does it follow (curiosity, benefit, pain, social proof)?
- What is the sub-headline doing - expanding, qualifying, or creating urgency?
- What emotion are they triggering in the first 5 seconds?

## 2. Problem & Pain Agitation
- What specific pain points do they call out?
- What language do they use to describe the problem?
- How do they make the reader feel the cost of inaction?

## 3. Solution & Mechanism
- How do they introduce their solution?
- What is their unique mechanism or proprietary framework?
- How do they differentiate from other solutions in the market?

## 4. Offer Stack
- List every component of the offer (core product, bonuses, extras)
- What value do they assign to each component?
- What is the total stacked value vs actual price?
- How do they frame the price (anchoring, comparison, ROI)?

## 5. Objection Handling
- What objections do they address and where on the page?
- Do they use FAQ sections, guarantee sections, or inline handling?
- What objections are they NOT addressing?

## 6. Social Proof Placement
- Where do testimonials appear relative to the offer and CTA?
- What type of proof do they use (results, transformation, authority)?
- How specific are the testimonials (vague praise vs concrete outcomes)?

## 7. CTA Strategy
- How many CTAs are on the page and what do they say?
- What micro-commitments lead up to the main CTA?
- Is there urgency or scarcity built in? How?

## 8. What I Can Steal & Improve
- What structural elements work well that I should adapt for [YOUR OFFER]?
- Where is this page weak and how can I do it better?
- What messaging angles are they missing that my audience cares about?

Be specific. Quote exact copy from the page where it illustrates a technique.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Headline formula identification with emotional trigger analysis
- Complete pain/desire framework they are using
- Full offer stack breakdown with pricing psychology tactics
- Objection handling inventory and gaps
- CTA placement strategy with urgency mechanisms
- Specific elements you can adapt and improve for your own sales page

## Pro Tips
- Screenshot the page before running this - sales pages change frequently and you want a record
- Focus on the "What I Can Steal & Improve" section for immediate action items
- Run this on 2-3 competitor sales pages for the same type of offer to spot industry patterns
