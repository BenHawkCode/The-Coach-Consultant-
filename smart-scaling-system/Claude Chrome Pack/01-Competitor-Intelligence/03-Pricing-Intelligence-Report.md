# Prompt 03: Pricing Intelligence Report

**Category:** Competitor Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Competitor's pricing page
**ROI Level:** High | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Extracts every pricing detail from a competitor's page - tiers, inclusions, positioning, and psychology tactics. Gives you the intelligence to price your own offers confidently, knowing exactly where you sit in the market and how to justify premium positioning.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
Analyse this pricing page in detail. I want a full pricing intelligence report I can use to inform my own pricing strategy.

# My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My current offer: [YOUR OFFER]
- My current price point: [YOUR PRICE or "not yet set"]
- My target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]

# Pricing Analysis

## 1. Tier Structure
- How many pricing tiers exist?
- What is each tier called and what does the naming suggest about positioning?
- What is the price of each tier (monthly, annual, one-time)?
- Which tier are they pushing hardest (highlighted, "most popular", recommended)?

## 2. Feature Breakdown
- Create a comparison table of what is included at each tier
- What features are gated behind higher tiers?
- What is the actual differentiator between tiers (access, volume, support, speed)?

## 3. Pricing Psychology
- Are they using charm pricing (e.g. £97 vs £100)?
- Is there price anchoring (showing a higher "value" then discounting)?
- Do they offer annual vs monthly with a discount? What percentage?
- Is there a free tier or trial? What does it include?
- Any urgency or scarcity elements (limited spots, price increase warnings)?

## 4. Value Framing
- How do they justify the price (ROI claims, cost comparisons, time saved)?
- Do they use "per day" or "per month" breakdowns to minimise perceived cost?
- What guarantees or risk-reversal do they offer?

## 5. What's Missing
- What common features in [YOUR NICHE] are absent from all tiers?
- What objections does this pricing page fail to address?
- Where could a competitor (me) position against them on value?

## 6. Positioning Recommendations
- Based on this analysis, where should [YOUR BRAND] price relative to this competitor?
- What pricing structure would differentiate my offer?
- What value elements could I add that they do not include?

Format as a structured report with the comparison table in markdown.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Complete tier-by-tier breakdown with feature comparison table
- Pricing psychology tactics identified and explained
- Value framing and justification strategies they use
- Gaps in their pricing that you can exploit
- Specific pricing recommendations for your own offer
- Risk-reversal and guarantee analysis

## Pro Tips
- Use the Wayback Machine to check if they have changed pricing recently - price increases signal growing demand
- Run this on 3-5 competitors to build a full market pricing map before setting your own prices
- Pay attention to what they gate behind higher tiers - that tells you what customers value most
