# Prompt 05: Map Their Customer Journey

**Category:** Competitor Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Competitor's homepage (then navigate through their site)
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Medium

## What This Does
Maps the full customer journey from first landing on a competitor's site through to the point of purchase. Identifies every trust-building step, conversion mechanism, and friction point - giving you a clear picture of how they move strangers into buyers so you can build a smoother path.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
I'm going to navigate through this competitor's website. Starting from this homepage, analyse the customer journey they have built. Map every step a visitor takes from landing here to reaching a purchase or booking decision.

# My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My website: [YOUR WEBSITE]

# Customer Journey Mapping

## 1. First Impression (This Page)
- What does a first-time visitor see above the fold?
- What is the single clearest action they want you to take?
- How quickly can a visitor understand what this business does and who it is for?
- What emotion or thought does the page trigger first?

## 2. Navigation Architecture
- What are the main navigation items and what do they prioritise?
- What pages are they pushing visitors toward?
- What is buried or hard to find that should be more prominent?
- Is there a clear primary path vs secondary paths?

## 3. Trust-Building Sequence
- In what order do they introduce: problem, solution, proof, offer, CTA?
- Where do they place social proof, case studies, and credibility signals?
- How many touchpoints exist before they ask for a commitment?
- What content do they use to warm up cold visitors (blog, resources, free tools)?

## 4. Conversion Steps
- What is the primary conversion action? (buy, book, opt-in, apply)
- How many clicks from homepage to that conversion point?
- What information do they require at each step?
- Are there intermediate conversions (email opt-in, quiz, free resource)?

## 5. Friction Points
- Where might a visitor get confused, distracted, or drop off?
- Are there dead ends, broken flows, or unclear next steps?
- Is the mobile experience smooth or compromised?
- Where do they lose momentum in the journey?

## 6. Journey Comparison
- How does this journey compare to best practice in [YOUR NICHE]?
- What steps are they doing well that [YOUR BRAND] should adopt?
- What friction points can I avoid on [YOUR WEBSITE]?
- What is the ideal customer journey I should build based on this analysis?

Create a visual journey map using a numbered step-by-step flow, showing each stage from landing to conversion with notes on what happens at each step.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Above-the-fold first impression assessment
- Full navigation and site architecture analysis
- Trust-building sequence mapped in order
- Click-path analysis from homepage to conversion
- Friction points and drop-off risks identified
- Step-by-step journey map you can use to improve your own site

## Pro Tips
- Open the site in an incognito window to see it as a genuine first-time visitor
- Use this prompt on the homepage first, then navigate to their sales page and run Prompt 02 for a deeper layer
- Compare 2-3 competitor journeys to find the most effective flow pattern in your market
