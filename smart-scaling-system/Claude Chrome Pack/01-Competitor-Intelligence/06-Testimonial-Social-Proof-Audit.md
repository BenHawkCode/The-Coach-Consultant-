# Prompt 06: Testimonial & Social Proof Audit

**Category:** Competitor Intelligence
**Where to Use:** Competitor's testimonials, results, or case studies page
**ROI Level:** Medium | **Setup:** Easy

## What This Does
Extracts and categorises every piece of social proof on a competitor's page - testimonials, case studies, client logos, statistics, and credibility signals. Assesses the strength of their proof and reveals gaps where your social proof strategy can overtake them.

---

## Copy & Paste Prompt

```
Audit every piece of social proof on this page. I want a full inventory and quality assessment of how this competitor builds trust and credibility.

# My Context
- My niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- My brand: [YOUR BRAND]
- My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]

# Social Proof Audit

## 1. Testimonial Inventory
- List every testimonial on this page
- For each one, note: who said it (name, title, company), what result they claimed, how specific the outcome is
- Rate each testimonial: Strong (specific result + named person), Medium (positive but vague), Weak (generic praise, no name)

## 2. Case Studies & Results
- Are there detailed case studies? Summarise each one
- What metrics or outcomes do they highlight (revenue, time saved, growth percentage)?
- Do they show before/after transformations?
- How credible are the results (verifiable, screenshot evidence, named clients)?

## 3. Trust Signals
- Client logos or "as seen in" media logos - list them all
- Certifications, awards, or partnership badges
- Specific numbers used (clients served, years in business, revenue generated)
- Any guarantees or risk-reversal statements

## 4. Social Proof Placement Strategy
- Where on the page does each type of proof appear?
- Is proof placed strategically near CTAs and pricing?
- What pattern do they follow (proof before offer, proof after objection, proof throughout)?

## 5. Quality Assessment
- Overall social proof strength: Strong / Moderate / Weak - and why
- What types of proof are they missing? (video testimonials, specific numbers, before/after, industry authority)
- What objections does their social proof fail to address?
- Are there any red flags (stock photos, suspiciously perfect results, no verifiable details)?

## 6. How to Beat Their Social Proof
- What types of testimonials should [YOUR BRAND] collect to outperform this?
- What specific results or metrics would be most compelling for [YOUR AUDIENCE]?
- What social proof formats would stand out in [YOUR NICHE] that this competitor is not using?
- Give me a social proof collection plan - what to ask clients and how to present it.

Quote exact testimonial text from the page where relevant.
```

---

## What You'll Get Back
- Complete inventory of every testimonial with quality ratings
- Case study and results breakdown with credibility assessment
- Full trust signal catalogue (logos, badges, numbers)
- Placement strategy analysis showing how they use proof to convert
- Honest quality assessment with identified gaps
- Actionable plan for building stronger social proof for your own brand

## Pro Tips
- Weak social proof from a competitor is a massive opportunity - if their testimonials are vague, yours being specific will instantly position you as more credible
- Look for what their testimonials do NOT mention - those blind spots often reveal pain points they are not solving
- Use the social proof collection plan output to create a client feedback form you can send immediately
