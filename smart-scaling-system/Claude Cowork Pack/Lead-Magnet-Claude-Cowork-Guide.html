How It Works

Give It a Task. Get Back a Result.

Cowork is built around one simple idea: describe the outcome you want, review Claude's plan, approve it, and come back to finished work. Here is the process.

Step 1

Describe Your Outcome

Tell Claude what you need. Not a prompt. Not a question. A task. "Build me a client report from this month's data." "Create a presentation for Friday's meeting." "Organise my project files by client."

Review the Plan

Your First Week

What Your Business Looks Like After 7 Days

You do not need to learn Cowork all at once. Here is exactly what to do in your first week to see real results immediately.

Day 1

Download & Set Your Folders

Install the Claude Desktop app, open Cowork, and grant access to your key business folders. Set your global instructions with your brand name, voice, and preferences. Time: 15 minutes.

Day 2

Your First Real Task

Give Claude a real task from your to-do list. "Organise this folder." "Create a report from this data." "Build a presentation for this meeting." Watch it plan, execute, and deliver.

Day 3

Build Your First Deliverable

Ask Claude to create something you would normally spend hours on. A client report, a financial summary, a proposal document. Review the output and see the quality for yourself.

Day 4

Connect Your First Integration

Link Google Calendar, Gmail, or Slack. Let Claude start working across your existing tools instead of in isolation. This is where the real power begins.

Day 5

Schedule Your First Recurring Task

Use /schedule to set up a weekly report, a daily briefing, or a Monday morning planning summary. From this day forward, that task happens without you.

Day 6

Try Computer Use

Let Claude handle a screen-based task. Fill in a form, navigate a tool, or complete a workflow across multiple apps. Experience the difference between a chatbot and an agent.

Day 7

Review & Scale

Look at what Claude handled this week. Calculate the hours saved. Plan which tasks to delegate next week. By now, you are not asking "does this work?" You are asking "what else can it do?"
