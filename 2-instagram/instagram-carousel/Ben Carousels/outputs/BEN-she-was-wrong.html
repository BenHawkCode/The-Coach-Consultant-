Slide 3 of 10

At 18, three years after my auntie had hung herself, a counsellor told me I had depression.

She put a label on me and sent me on my way. No deeper investigation. No follow-up.

Just "you're depressed." And I believed her. For years.

Still no one actually looking underneath at what was really going on.

Slide 5 of 10

At 26, I was told I had performance anxiety.

At this point I had a collection of labels and not a single actual solution. Just professionals giving me words and sending me home.

Slide 6 of 10

At 29, I'd had enough. I stopped accepting other people's answers and went out on my own route.

Got my own diagnosis. Paid for it myself. Pursued it relentlessly until I finally understood what was actually happening inside my own head.

Slide 7 of 10

ADHD. A chemical imbalance. Unprocessed trauma that had been sitting there for over a decade.

That was the real answer. Not depression. Not anxiety. Not ghosts.

The real, actual answer that nobody had bothered to find for thirteen years.

Slide 8 of 10

The day I got that diagnosis felt like a new beginning.

Not because it fixed everything overnight. But because for the first time, I understood myself.

And when you understand the problem properly, you can actually solve it.
