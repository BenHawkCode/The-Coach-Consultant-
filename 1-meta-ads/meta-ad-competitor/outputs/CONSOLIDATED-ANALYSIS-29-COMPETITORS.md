## 🏆 TOP PERFORMING HOOK PATTERNS

### 1. Metric/Result Hooks (Alex Hormozi, Leila Hormozi, Sam Ovens)

**Pattern:**
```
[Specific Number] + [Target Audience] + [Achieving Result]
```

**Examples:**
- "👉 Join 171.9k community builders collectively earning over $1 billion per year on the Skool platform TODAY."
- "$100M Money Models shows how to get more customers to spend more money with you, in less time, over and over again."
- "SaaS companies on average see a 15%+ increase in ad revenue from HYROS Ai Attribution."

**Why It Works:** Social proof + specificity = credibility

---

### 2. Statement Hooks (Billy Gene, Gary Vee, Frank Kern)

**Pattern:**
```
[Here's how/what] + [Simple Process] + [Clear Outcome]
```

**Examples:**
- "Here's the biz plan: 1. Business owners need content. 2. They'd rather pay someone to do it. 3. Use A.I. like..."
- "Everything in this post is written assuming that you have something to sell, that people want it, and that it's good."
- "Listen to the end … promise … listen deeply .. it matters … once you get "it" … your life gets better"

**Why It Works:** Removes friction, breaks down complexity into simple steps

---

### 3. Question Hooks (Dan Martell, Gary Vaynerchuk)

**Pattern:**
```
[Relatable Situation] + [Question that triggers self-reflection]
```

**Examples:**
- "Starting a service business? Make this one 45 second video and send it to every person in your contact list"
- "Is forced diversity in Hollywood hurting or helping the entertainment industry?"

**Why It Works:** Engages curiosity, makes reader pause and think

---

### 4. Pain Point Hooks (Sarah Levinger, Nick Shackelford)

**Pattern:**
```
[Most people struggle with X] → [Here's the real problem] → [Here's the solution]
```

**Examples:**
- "Most brands don't have an ad problem — they have a creative problem. At CreativeCon, you'll learn how top brands..."
- "We've teamed up with the unstoppable Nick Shackelford to bring you a tactical countdown to your biggest Q4 ever."

**Why It Works:** Identifies the REAL problem (not surface level), positions you as the solution

---

### 5. Bold Statement Hooks (Russell Brunson, Frank Kern, Rob Moore)

**Pattern:**
```
[Controversial/Unexpected Statement] + [Why it matters]
```

**Examples:**
- "How To Get Consulting Clients Using Ugly Ads. I got served this ad and I thought it didn't work... Until I looked behind the scenes."
- "Watch Russell Brunson's interview on me about the newest way to convert clients online without any marketing skills required"

**Why It Works:** Pattern interrupt, challenges conventional wisdom

## 💰 OFFER POSITIONING PATTERNS

### Lead Magnet Offers (Most Common)
- Free Training/Masterclass
- Free Guide/PDF
- Free Trial (14-day standard)
- Free Workshop/Challenge

### Mid-Ticket Offers
- $97-$297 courses
- Monthly memberships ($9-$99/mo)
- Live events ($100-$500)

### High-Ticket Positioning
- Book a Call (consultation)
- Apply Now (qualification-based)
- Limited spots (scarcity)

---

## 🎨 CREATIVE PATTERNS

### What Top Performers Do:
1. **Keep it simple** - No fancy graphics, just clear message
2. **Lead with value** - What's in it for them?
3. **Use social proof** - Numbers, testimonials, authority
4. **Short copy wins** - 2-3 sentences max before "see more"
5. **Test multiple angles** - Same offer, different hooks

### What They DON'T Do:
- ❌ Generic "scale your business" promises
- ❌ Overly salesy language
- ❌ Long paragraphs before the hook
- ❌ Vague benefits without specifics

## 🚀 ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS FOR BEN HAWKSWORTH

### For The Coach Consultant Brand:

**1. Hook Formula to Use:**
```
Statement (60%) + Metric/Result (20%) + Question (20%)
```

**2. CTA Strategy:**
- **Awareness Stage:** No CTA (pure value)
- **Consideration Stage:** Soft CTA (Learn more)
- **Decision Stage:** Hard CTA (Book a Call)

**3. Offer Positioning:**
- Lead Magnet: "Free Coaching Scorecard" or "Free Systems Audit"
- Mid-Ticket: £297 course or membership
- High-Ticket: 1-1 consulting via application

**4. Copy Style:**
- British English throughout
- Direct, no-nonsense tone (Yorkshire influence)
- Short sentences, one per line
- No AI jargon ("Here's the thing", "Let's dive in")

**5. Testing Priority:**
```
Hook Type A: Statement
"Right so here's what I see constantly working for coaches hitting £10K months..."

Hook Type B: Metric/Result
"147 coaches, consultants and service providers used this exact system to add £5K-£15K/month"

Hook Type C: Question
"Why do some coaches hit £10K months while others stay stuck at £2K? It's not talent..."
```
